* The Law of \equal

#+begin_verse

An expression $(= \textit{X}\space\textit{from}\space\textit{to})$ is a type if \\
$\textit{X}$ is a type, \\
$\textit{from}$ is an $\textit{X}$ \\
and \\
$\textit{to}$ is an $\textit{X}$.

#+end_verse

Note that from and to don’t have to be the same, just of the same /type/. For
example:

#+begin_example

(= Atom ’Hello ’World)

#+end_example

is a type despite that =’Hello= \ne =’World=.
